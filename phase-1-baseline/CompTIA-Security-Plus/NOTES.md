## About This Certification

CompTIA Security+ (SY0-701) is the baseline DoD 8140/8570 certification required for all privileged access roles in the Department of Defense. It covers foundational cybersecurity concepts across threat management, network security, identity and access management, cryptography, and risk management. It is vendor-neutral, globally recognized, and the mandatory first step before any DoD cyber role. Passing Security+ satisfies IAT Level II and IAM Level II requirements under DoD 8140.

***

## How the exam is graded and structured:&#x20;

* 90 Minutes, maximum of 90 questions.
* Passing score: 750 on a scale of 100-900

The exam is divided in **Domains** that and each domains represents a certain % of the total grade for the exam.&#x20;

| Domain                                                    | % of Exam                                                |

## Domain 1 — General Security Concepts

_Controls, cryptography basics, authentication, and security fundamentals. Foundation for everything else._

### Chapter 1 — Mastering Security Basics

_CIA Triad, control categories (technical/managerial/operational/physical), control types (preventive/detective/corrective), logging and monitoring, SIEM._

***

## Domain 2 — Threats, Vulnerabilities & Mitigations

_Threat actors, attack types, malware, social engineering, vulnerability scanning, and how to defend against them._

### Chapter 6 — Comparing Threats, Vulnerabilities, and Common Attacks

_Threat actor types and motivations, attack surfaces, malware categories (viruses/worms/trojans/ransomware), social engineering tactics, threat intelligence sources._

### Chapter 7 — Protecting Against Advanced Attacks

_DoS/DDoS, on-path attacks, DNS attacks, replay attacks, injection attacks (SQL/XSS/LDAP), buffer overflows, secure coding concepts, automation and scripting._

***

## Domain 3 — Security Architecture

_Network design, segmentation, hardening, virtualization, embedded systems, and physical security._

### Chapter 3 — Exploring Network Technologies and Tools

_OSI model, protocols and use cases, switches, routers, firewalls, network segmentation, security zones, screened subnets, Zero Trust, SASE._

### Chapter 4 — Securing Your Network

_IDS/IPS, VPNs (IPsec/SSL/L2TP), NAC, PAP/CHAP/RADIUS/TACACS+, AAA protocols._

### Chapter 5 — Securing Hosts and Data

_Virtualization, containerization, VM security, endpoint hardening, mobile device management, IoT/OT/ICS/SCADA, embedded systems._

### Chapter 9 — Implementing Controls to Protect Assets

_Physical security controls, asset management, RAID, redundancy, fault tolerance, backups, business continuity, disaster recovery._

***

## Domain 4 — Security Operations

_IAM, risk management, incident response, forensics, and policy implementation._

### Chapter 2 — Understanding Identity and Access Management

_Authentication factors, MFA, SSO, LDAP, SAML, OAuth, RBAC, DAC, MAC, attribute-based access control, privileged access management._

### Chapter 8 — Using Risk Management Tools

_Risk types and identification, vulnerability scanning, penetration testing methodology, network traffic analysis, NIST frameworks, benchmarks and configuration guides._

### Chapter 11 — Implementing Policies to Mitigate Risks

_Change management, data classification and protection, data retention and sanitization, incident response planning and process, digital forensics, SOAR._

***

## Domain 5 — Security Program Management & Oversight

_Cryptography, PKI, certificates, and compliance._

### Chapter 10 — Understanding Cryptography and PKI

_Hashing (MD5/SHA/HMAC), password attacks and defenses, symmetric vs asymmetric encryption, AES/3DES, block vs stream ciphers, PKI, certificates, certificate management, key escrow._
